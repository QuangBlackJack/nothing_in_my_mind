1. Explain the core game mechanic of the game.
Beatstar's core gameplay mechanics largely revolve around interacting with on-screen tiles to play music and keep to the beat. Here are some key points of the gameplay mechanism that I learned when experiencing the game Beatstar:
  • The game requires players to press and hold at the right time. The player needs to tap on the music tiles as they move across the screen on 3 rows. Pressing needs to be done at the right time to create perfect notes.
  • The game requires the player to select music and unlock new music through Levels. The game offers a variety of tracks that can potentially be unlocked through the player's level. Players can unlock music by opening a case containing random music. Players can choose their favorite track and try to get the highest score by making as many ‚Äúperfect taps‚Äù as possible.
  • Player level will directly affect scores and combo effects (effects will appear when many perfect taps are performed through my experience). Players receive points based on pressing at the right time and keeping in rhythm. Correct performance and not letting the music tiles fall will determine their score. If the player lets the brick pass, the player will have to reset the recent part of the song that they have missed.
  • The difficulty of the game will gradually increase corresponding to the player's level. Players with higher levels must challenge with greater difficulty (‚ÄúWith great power comes great responsibility‚Äù ‚Äì Uncle Ben).
2. Explain and compare how music is integrated with the gameplay of the game.
Integrating music into games is a great way to influence gamers' inspiration and psychology (I also did this in my university game project). Integrating music into Beatstar's gameplay is no longer strange for music-themed games, but for me, it still has its own charm. Here is my opinion on how this game integrates music:
  • Beatstar has chosen music methodically and paid attention to users' tastes. Through observing the list of songs in the playlist, I noticed that the game provided much popular and diverse music from many different genres. These tracks are chosen because they bring familiarity or have attractive melodies that motivate the player. However, if I'm not mistaken, perhaps this application is mainly aimed at international users based on the fact that the application only provides songs of the European and American genre, maybe other Korean songs but I only see Gangnam style (or maybe my knowledge of Korean music is quite limited).
  • Beatstar can be considered an upgraded version of the legendary "Don't Tap the White Tile" when it divided the Music into Music tiles. Each track is divided into music tiles that move across the screen according to a certain rule. In my experience, each tile corresponds to a note, a small part of the music, or a word that the singer sings in the song.
  • Beatstar Moves music tiles according to the beat of the song. Moving the music brick across the screen is synchronized with the rhythm of the music. Players need to tap or hold on the boxes containing the music tiles at the right time to create the correct sound and keep in tune with the beat.

4. Describe the style and aesthetic of the game. Explain how the game achieves that aesthetic through its design, art, and audio.
As for style, the Beatstar gameplay screen has a simple and minimalist design style, focusing mainly on music tiles moving on the screen. The user interface is designed to make the game intuitive and easy to use. In terms of aesthetics, music tiles, and motion effects create a colorful and lively screen. The player's vision is stimulated through the blend of music tiles, paths, and outstanding effects.
Players need to tap on the boxes to press exactly to the beat and hold in time with the music. Accuracy and synchronization with the beat are the keys to getting through songs (just like a dancer must dance to the exact rhythm and beat of the music). The sound of musical notes is combined with the smooth movement of music tiles, creating a harmonious musical and visual experience. Sound effects combined with images enhance the player's aesthetic experience.
